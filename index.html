CatDT

Composable Digital Twin Architecture for Supply Chain Redesign

A design-first structural layer for modeling and redesigning supply chains under nearshoring and volatility.

Founder: Juan Orendain, PhD
Email: juan@catdt.com
The problem

Supply chains are being redesigned at a structural level—driven by nearshoring, geopolitical risk, and the fragility exposed during COVID. Yet most planning and optimization stacks assume a largely fixed network. When the network itself changes (suppliers, lanes, facilities, modes), organizations face brittle tools, siloed models, and inconsistent outputs across planning, simulation, and execution.

What CatDT is

CatDT is a design-first digital twin engine for supply chains. It introduces a compositional architecture that lets teams assemble, modify, and evaluate supply-chain structures modularly—so that changes in the network propagate consistently across scenario design, simulation, optimization, and operational interfaces.

CatDT is built to integrate above existing enterprise systems rather than replace them—connecting to ERP/TMS/WMS and analytics stacks while providing the missing structural “design layer” for end-to-end coherence.

Why now

This is a structural moment: regionalization and nearshoring are forcing companies to re-architect supply chains across North America. At the same time, modern modeling and software infrastructure make compositional system design feasible in practice, enabling a new class of digital twins focused on structural redesign—not just monitoring or optimizing within a static network.

Initial focus

CatDT initially targets supply chain network design and strategic planning teams—especially in organizations that face repeated redesign cycles and need consistent scenario evaluation across multiple tools. The near-term goal is to define pilot engagements where CatDT can reduce model rework, improve scenario throughput, and increase structural predictability in redesign decisions.

Status

CatDT is an early-stage product developed by DCat Technologies. The project is founder-led and currently engaging industry contacts for exploratory discussions and pilot definition. The immediate objective is to secure a small number of pilot partners and convert those pilots into reusable case studies and product requirements.
